# Bitcoin

Bitcoin (BTC) is a decentralized digital currency launched in 2009 by the pseudonymous
Satoshi Nakamoto. It runs on a proof-of-work blockchain where miners compete to append
blocks roughly every ten minutes. The total supply is capped at 21 million coins, and the
block subsidy halves approximately every four years in an event called the halving, which
reduces new issuance over time.

Bitcoin is widely treated as a store of value or "digital gold" rather than a high-throughput
payments network. Its base layer handles a limited number of transactions per second, so
scaling solutions such as the Lightning Network operate as a second layer for fast, cheap
payments. Key risk factors for BTC include regulatory changes, macro liquidity conditions,
and concentration of mining hash power.
