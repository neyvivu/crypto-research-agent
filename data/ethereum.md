# Ethereum

Ethereum (ETH) is a programmable blockchain launched in 2015 that supports smart contracts:
self-executing code deployed on-chain. It is the settlement layer for most decentralized
finance (DeFi) and non-fungible token (NFT) activity. In 2022, Ethereum transitioned from
proof-of-work to proof-of-stake in an upgrade known as the Merge, sharply lowering its energy
use and changing ETH issuance dynamics.

Validators stake ETH to secure the network and earn rewards; a portion of transaction fees is
burned under EIP-1559, which can make ETH deflationary during periods of high activity.
Ethereum scales primarily through Layer 2 rollups (optimistic and zero-knowledge) that batch
transactions off-chain and post proofs back to mainnet. Risks include smart-contract bugs,
rollup centralization, and competition from alternative Layer 1 chains.
